'Helper' Case Study

Problem

Elders struggle to connect with smartphone. They only use limited features on the smartphone due to lack of interest and knowledge in tech.

I’d like to explore how I might improve the smartphone experience of elders.

Solution

I used human-centered design approach to create a ‘Helper’ app to improve elders’ smartphone experience.

Secondary Research- Concerns for age 50 or older

Fact #1 Privacy and security

- Only 18% are confident that their data are kept private online.
- They don’t take proactive steps to secure their data.
- A little over half use a passcode.

Fact #2

We shouldn’t underestimate by stating that all the elders are avoiding new technology.

Fact #3 Barriers

- Have declining eyesight and hearing, limited income may keep seniors away from the latest tech features.
- They prefer large, affordable devices with lower-resolution screens, so icons and touch targets are larger
and easier to hit.

Testing

Usability Test #1

Problems

• Users are likely to skip the ‘How it works’ button
• Difficult to notice the ‘Help’ box
• Need explanation when setting up ‘Password Keeper’
• Confuse users when saving passwords

Solutions

• Include onboarding design
• Grey out the background for users to focus on the ‘Help’ box and include arrow or guide notes to ‘Help’ box
• Include short explanationin the first page
• Save password one by one, and put ‘Add Another Password’ in the button

Usability Test #2

Problems

• ‘Start’ button expects user to the main page instead of tutorial
• First page of ‘Onboarding Design’ is not clear
• ‘Previous Saved Password’ is not necessary

Solutions

• Rewrite ‘Start,’ and add back ‘Tutorial’ button again
• Increase the size of the box, font, and options of helper
• Delete the ‘Previous Saved Password’
